Resources

Here are some resources that I have gathered which can be helpful for econ grad students and to you as a mentor.

SMU specific

IMPORTANT: If you, or any other students, have any issue like hard time finding housing, health related, problems with professors, etc, you can submit a CCC report or a BERT report.

These reports help the Student Advocacy and Support (OSUS) to help those students in need. These reports do not always end in an intervention of the reported student or staff, but any information can help the team to understand the problem and find a way to fix it.

  • The is a FREE food pantry called The Shop where you can grab grocery food when you are strugguling to eat.
  • The Dr. Bob Smith Center offers free counseling services.
    • They also offer a bunch of workshops or group counceling like "Overcoming Perfectionism Workshop", "Anxiety Toolbox Group", and "Grief and Loss Group" at no charge.
  • The Graduate Writing Center has dedicated consultants to help you at any stage of the writing process, from brainstorming to the final draft.
  • The ISSS has a page dedicated to getting a driver’s license, applying for a Social Security Number, taxes, etc.
  • Available SMU grants:
    • Dedman College Interdisciplinary Institute's "Graduate Student Summer Fellowship".
    • Moody School's "Provost’s Summer Research Grants". (Summer 2024 Call)
